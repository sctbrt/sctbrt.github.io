What I fix

Where performance slips in plain sight.

Most businesses don’t have a “people problem.” They have a system that asks people to remember too much. I remove friction at the counter, in handoffs, and in the daily routines that quietly drive costs.

Front line

Shorter lines, fewer voids — fewer “what do we do here?” moments during peak.

Back of house

Cleaner handoffs — predictable prep, labeling, and staging so teams stop improvising.

Standards

Less comping and redos — reduce errors by reducing decision points.

Customer experience

Confidence at the counter — service feels calm, even when it’s busy.

My approach

Reduce cognitive load. Increase reliability.

I use a simple behavioral lens: if a process relies on memory, it will fail under stress. We redesign the environment so the right action is the easiest action — with cues, defaults, and clean rules.

Step 1

Observe the real work: where staff hesitate, ask, or backtrack.

Step 2

Map decisions: reduce choices, simplify menus, standardize handoffs.

Step 3

Design cues: signage, scripts, layouts, checklists, and “one right way.”

Step 4

Train + refine in the field: make the system stick with light iterations.
